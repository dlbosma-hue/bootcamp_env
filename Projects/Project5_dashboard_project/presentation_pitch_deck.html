Where Does the AI Live?

Three connected layers.
Nothing is hidden.

Three layers. Each one does a specific job. The data flows left to right: from raw check-in records to a Slack message in your coach's pocket every morning.

Layer 1: Data
Kaggle datasets
167,000 real workout sessions (Endomondo) + 4,000 labelled gym members (Model Fitness). Processed into 1,059 member risk profiles.
Python script
Calculates sessions/week, days inactive, sport variety. Labels every member low / medium / high risk using validated thresholds.
Tableau dashboard
Visual output: all 1,059 member risk profiles in one view. Used for management reporting and coach briefings.

Layer 2: AI Brain
LangChain + GPT-4o-mini

LangSmith monitoring
Every AI call is logged, scored, and auditable. Chleo can see every output and why it was rated high or low. Nothing happens without a record.
Lives: OpenAI cloud + LangSmith cloud

How the AI Spots Them

Three numbers.
Four to six weeks early.

The AI reads signals already in your check-in data. No new sensors, no surveys.

The AI does not use a black box model. It reads three numbers: sessions/week, days inactive, sport variety. Applies validated thresholds. Every flag can be explained in plain language: this member trains half as often as they used to and has not shown up in three months.

Why this combination works: Frequency drops first. Then recency grows. Then variety narrows. All three moving together is the pattern. Each signal alone is noise. Together they are a 4–6 week early warning.
Transparency: LangSmith

"But how do I know
the AI isn't making stuff up?"

Every AI call is logged, scored, and explained. This is what that looks like. LangSmith is a monitoring platform connected directly to the AI: every question asked, every answer given, every score received. Nothing goes unexamined.

1
The risk score is explainable
Every member's churn label comes from three numbers you can point to: sessions/week, days inactive, sport variety. Not a neural network. Three thresholds. A coach can verify it in 10 seconds.
2
Every AI output is logged
LangSmith records every single LLM call: what was asked, what data was given, what the AI said. Every output has a permanent audit record.
3
Every output is scored
Three independent evaluators score every AI output on actionability, relevance, and clarity. Scores appear in LangSmith in green or red. Quality becomes visible.
4
The human decides
The Slack alert is a recommendation, not an action. AI flags. Coach reads. Coach decides whether to call. At no point does the system contact members or act without a human.
