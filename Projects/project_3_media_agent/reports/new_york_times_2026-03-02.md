## 10. Recommendations

1. **Implement mandatory diversity tracking for bylines and community coverage** — The New York Times editorial leadership should commission a real-time byline diversity dashboard (tracking gender, race, and disability status of authors where voluntarily disclosed) with quarterly public reporting. This should be operational within six months, owned by the Managing Editor's office in coordination with the DEI team.

2. **Establish a sourcing diversity protocol for all major beats** — The standards desk should develop and enforce a sourcing checklist requiring journalists on political, military, and international affairs beats to demonstrate that at least one marginalised community voice or subject-matter expert from an underrepresented group has been sought for inclusion in each piece. This protocol should be piloted within 90 days on the foreign affairs and national security desks.

3. **Integrate community impact framing into conflict and policy coverage** — The foreign and national security editors should introduce editorial guidance requiring conflict coverage to include, where relevant, the documented impact on civilian populations, women, ethnic minorities, and displaced communities. Training workshops drawing on best-practice frameworks from the Dart Centre for Journalism and Trauma should be delivered to relevant teams within six months.

4. **Conduct a longitudinal inclusivity audit with expanded scope** — Media Diversity Watch recommends the New York Times commission or participate in a 90-day rolling inclusivity audit — covering bylines, sourcing, portrayal, and language — to establish a reliable baseline against which progress can be measured. This audit should be conducted by an independent body and results made publicly available, with the first report published no later than Q4 2026.
