## 10. Recommendations

1. **Commission a full-text content audit (Editorial Leadership — within 6 months):** The RSS teaser methodology used in this report cannot assess portrayal depth, in-article sourcing, or body-text language patterns. NPR's editorial team, in partnership with an external diversity research organisation, should commission a full-text analysis of at least 6 months of published content across all major beats to produce a reliable baseline for inclusivity assessment.

2. **Implement a community coverage tracking dashboard (Data and Analytics team — within 3 months):** NPR should establish an internal tracking system that monitors the proportion of published articles covering LGBTQ+, disability, race, and gender topics on a rolling monthly basis. This would allow editorial leadership to identify coverage gaps in real time and make commissioning decisions accordingly, rather than discovering gaps retrospectively in external audits.

3. **Review sourcing practices for marginalised community stories (Standards and Practices Editor — within 6 months):** Drawing on industry benchmarks that flag the tendency to use institutional outside voices to speak *about* communities rather than amplifying community members as experts, NPR should audit sourcing patterns in stories about racial minorities, LGBTQ+ people, people with disabilities, and women. Where gaps are identified, editors should establish clear guidance on prioritising community voices in sourcing hierarchies.

4. **Conduct a byline diversity review (Human Resources and Editorial Leadership — within 9 months):** The current audit identified 28 unique authors but had no data on the demographic composition of NPR's authorial workforce. NPR should conduct and publish a byline diversity review disaggregated by gender, race, and disability status, benchmarked against both the US population and industry peers, and set measurable targets for improvement where gaps are found.
