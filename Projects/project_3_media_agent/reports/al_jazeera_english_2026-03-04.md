## 10. Recommendations

1. **Restore byline attribution across all digital content — Editorial leadership, within 30 days.** The complete absence of bylines across 25 reviewed articles is an immediate transparency failure. Al Jazeera English's editorial leadership should audit its CMS and RSS feed configuration to identify why author data is not being published, and reinstate full byline attribution as a non-negotiable editorial standard. This is a prerequisite for any meaningful future diversity audit of authorship demographics.

2. **Commission a full LGBTQ+ coverage audit using 90-day article archive — Diversity and Inclusion team, within 60 days.** Given the zero representation in the current sample and the structural context of Qatari state funding, Al Jazeera English should proactively audit its LGBTQ+ coverage record across a larger dataset. The audit should assess volume, framing, sourcing, and language against GLAAD and Stonewall media guidelines, and the findings should be published transparently.

3. **Establish minimum quarterly representation benchmarks for marginalised communities — Editorial strategy team, within 90 days.** Media Diversity Watch benchmarks recommend that inclusive outlets track story selection data by community on a rolling basis. Al Jazeera English should introduce internal targets for coverage of women, LGBTQ+ people, people with disabilities, and racial minorities — with quarterly reporting to editorial leadership — to move from reactive to proactive representation management.

4. **Implement expert-sourcing guidelines that position community members as credentialled authorities — Training and standards team, within 6 months.** The research findings indicate a pattern of communities being quoted about personal experience rather than positioned as subject-matter experts. Al Jazeera English should develop sourcing guidelines — aligned with the Conscious Style Guide and BBC Editorial Standards on diversity — that explicitly require journalists to seek expert voices from within the communities they cover, rather than defaulting to external or institutional spokespersons.
