## 10. Recommendations

1. **Implement a mandatory byline transparency policy (Owner: CNN Editorial Standards Desk; Timeline: Within 60 days).** All published articles — including video-led and wire-sourced content — must carry an identifiable author or contributing team byline. This is a prerequisite for any meaningful ongoing authorship diversity monitoring and should be audited quarterly against byline gender and demographic targets.

2. **Conduct an urgent review of content classification and tagging systems (Owner: CNN Digital Product and Editorial Technology teams; Timeline: Within 30 days).** The misclassification of a spy balloon article as LGBTQ+ content indicates a fault in automated or manual tagging workflows. An internal audit should identify the source of the error and establish human editorial oversight of community tags before publication.

3. **Establish and publish diversity coverage targets for marginalised communities (Owner: CNN Executive Editorial Leadership; Timeline: Within 90 days).** CNN should set transparent, measurable targets for the proportion of published stories that substantively feature racial and ethnic minority communities, disabled people, LGBTQ+ individuals, and women as experts or protagonists — not merely as victims or incidental subjects. Progress should be reported publicly on a semi-annual basis.

4. **Build and maintain a structured diverse source database (Owner: CNN Diversity, Equity and Inclusion team in partnership with the Newsgathering desk; Timeline: Pilot within 120 days, full rollout within 12 months).** Drawing on Media Diversity Watch benchmarks — which show men are quoted approximately three times more often than women as expert sources — CNN should develop a searchable internal database of expert sources from underrepresented communities, including racial minorities, disabled people, LGBTQ+ individuals, and women, with particular emphasis on fields such as politics, technology, and business where sourcing gaps are most acute.
